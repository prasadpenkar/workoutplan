Part 1 — The Plan

Core principles

Progressive overload drives muscle gain — each week add a little weight, a rep, or a set. Hit the top of a rep range with good form on all sets? Add ~2.5–5% next time. Train in the 8–12 rep range for muscle, resting 60–90 sec between sets. Stamina builds through progressively longer cardio and intervals (Days 3 & 6). At 44, recovery matters — 7–9 hrs sleep, and don't skip the rest day. And always: form over weight.

Day 6 — Endurance + Accessory

Steady-state cardio (30–40 min): treadmill, cycle, or row at a conversational-to-moderate pace; add 2–5 min or more distance each week.
Accessory circuit (~15 min) — 3 rounds: face pulls ×15 · DB shrugs ×15 · cable curl ×12 · triceps pushdown ×12 · calf raise ×20.

Day 7 — Rest / Active Recovery

No lifting. 20–30 min easy walk, 10–15 min stretching/mobility. Part of the program, not optional.

Progression over 8–12 weeks

Wk 1–2: learn movements, set baselines, lower rep end. Wk 3–6: add weight/reps wherever form holds; push cardio. Wk 7–8: deload if beaten up (−40% volume), then push again. Track everything — if a number stalls for weeks, change something (food, sleep, or the exercise).

Safety reminders

Warm up fully. Stop on sharp joint pain (muscle burn is fine). Breathe out on exertion, never hold your breath under load. Chest pain, dizziness, or unusual breathlessness → stop and seek medical advice.

Part 2 — Form Guide

For each: setup, execution, key cues, and common mistakes — with a posture diagram for the form-critical lifts.

Two universal rules: ① Control the lowering (2–3 sec down) on every lift — that's where most growth and joint protection live. ② Breathe out on the hard part, in on the return.

Cardio

Treadmill (jog / incline walk)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Start slow, step on, stand tall, eyes forward. Incline walk at 5–10% briskly, or jog landing mid-foot under your hips. Cue: don't hold the handrails — let arms swing. Avoid: hunching the console, gripping rails, speed so high your stride breaks.

Stationary bike

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Seat height: a slight knee bend at the bottom of the stroke — not locked, not cramped.

Pedal in smooth full circles at moderate resistance, sit tall, relax the grip, knees tracking straight. Avoid: seat too low, rocking hips, white-knuckling the bars.

Rower

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Feet strapped, sit tall, light overhand grip. Sequence: legs → back → arms; return arms → back → legs. Cue: ~60% of power from the legs; handle moves in a straight horizontal line. Avoid: arms-first pulling, rounding the back, rushing the recovery.

Chest — pressing

Chest press machine / Incline chest press

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Seat set so handles align with mid-chest (incline: upper chest), feet flat, back on the pad. Press to nearly straight (no hard lock), return slowly to a gentle chest stretch. Cue: shoulder blades pulled back and down. Avoid: wrong seat height, elbows flaring to 90°, bouncing the stack.

Dumbbell bench press / Incline dumbbell press

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Top-down view: tuck the elbows to roughly 45°, don't let them flare straight out.

Kick the dumbbells to shoulder level lying back, feet flat. Press up and slightly together; lower slowly until elbows are about level with the torso. Avoid: banging dumbbells at the top, flaring elbows, hard lower-back arch.

Push-ups

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Body stays a straight plank line — no sagging hips, no piked butt. Elbows ~45°.

Hands slightly wider than shoulders. Lower chest to an inch off the floor, press up. Cue: squeeze glutes, brace core. Too hard? Hands on a bench.

Back — pulling

Lat pulldown / Wide-grip pulldown

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Pull the bar to the upper chest by driving the elbows down — not behind the neck.

Grip wider than shoulders, knees under the pad. Control back to a full stretch. Cue: pull with the elbows, not the hands. Avoid: heaving with momentum, behind-the-neck pulls, no full extension.

Seated cable / machine row

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Sit tall, feet braced, slight knee bend. Pull the handle to the lower ribs/belly, elbows straight back, squeeze the shoulder blades; return slowly. Cue: torso upright and still. Avoid: yanking with the lower back, shrugging, rounding forward.

Single-arm dumbbell row

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

One knee + same-side hand on a bench, flat back ~parallel to the floor, dumbbell hanging. Pull to the hip leading with the elbow; lower to a full stretch. Cue: keep the torso square. Avoid: rotating for momentum, shrugging, rounding the back.

Bent-over dumbbell row (Day 3 circuit)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Hinge at the hips with a flat back; row both dumbbells to the lower ribs.

Avoid: a rounded back, standing too upright (becomes a shrug), momentum.

Face pulls

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Rope on a pulley at head height, palms in, step back for tension. Pull toward the face, separating the hands so they end beside your ears, elbows high. Cue: lead with the elbows — great for shoulder health. Avoid: too much weight, dropping the elbows, jerking.

Shoulders

Dumbbell shoulder press (seated/standing)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Press straight up over the shoulders; keep ribs down so the lower back doesn't over-arch.

Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward, elbows under wrists. Press up to nearly straight, lower slowly to ear level. Avoid: over-arching, clanking at the top, pressing in front of the body.

Dumbbell lateral raise

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Stand tall, slight elbow bend, raise out to the sides to about shoulder height, lower slowly. Cue: lead with the elbows; light weight. Avoid: swinging, going far above shoulder height, too heavy.

Rear-delt fly (machine/cable)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Chest on the pad (reverse pec deck) or crossed cables. Sweep arms out and back in a wide arc, squeeze the rear shoulders, return slowly with a constant slight elbow bend. Avoid: bending/straightening arms to cheat, using traps, too heavy.

Dumbbell shrugs

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Dumbbells at the sides, shrug straight up toward the ears, pause, lower slowly. Cue: straight up and down — don't roll; arms stay straight. Avoid: rolling the shoulders, using the arms, head bobbing forward.

Arms

Dumbbell biceps curl

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Dumbbells at the sides, palms forward. Curl by bending the elbow, upper arm still, squeeze, lower slowly. Cue: elbows pinned to the sides. Avoid: swinging, elbows drifting forward, fast drop.

Hammer curl

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Same as the curl but palms face each other (neutral grip) throughout — hits the forearm and outer arm more. Same cues and mistakes.

Cable curl

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Low pulley with a bar, step back, palms up. Curl with elbows pinned, squeeze, lower slowly — control both directions. Avoid: leaning back to heave, elbows flaring/drifting.

Triceps pushdown

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

High pulley, rope/bar, elbows tucked, slight forward lean. Push to straight arms, squeeze; return only to forearms-parallel, elbows pinned. Cue: only the forearms move. Avoid: elbows flaring forward, using bodyweight, bouncing.

Overhead dumbbell triceps extension

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

One dumbbell in both hands overhead, arms extended. Lower behind the head by bending the elbows (upper arms vertical), press up. Cue: elbows point forward, stay close to the head. Avoid: elbows flaring, moving the upper arms, too heavy near the head.

Legs

Leg press machine

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Back flat on the pad, feet shoulder-width, mid-foot. Release safeties, lower to ~90° knee bend, press up without hard-locking. Cue: knees track over toes; lower back stays glued to the pad. Avoid: going so low the back rounds off the pad, hard lock-out, knees caving.

Hack squat machine

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Shoulders under the pads, back flat on the backrest, feet shoulder-width. Lower to ~90°, drive up through the heels. Cue: knees over toes, don't lock hard. Avoid: heels lifting, knees caving, bouncing.

Goblet squat (dumbbell)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Hold the weight at the chest, sit to about parallel, knees tracking over the toes, chest up.

Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Push the hips back, drive up through the heels. Avoid: heels rising, knees caving, rounded upper back, partial depth.

Bodyweight squat

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Same movement as the goblet squat with no weight, hands out front or on hips. Same cues and mistakes.

Walking lunges (dumbbell/bodyweight)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Both knees bend to ~90°; front shin stays vertical (knee over the foot, not way past the toes).

Step forward into the lunge, back knee nearly to the floor; push off the front foot into the next step. Avoid: steps too short, leaning forward, rushing.

Leg curl machine (hamstrings)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Pad just above the heels. Curl the heels toward the glutes, squeeze, lower slowly. Cue: hips stay down; movement only at the knee. Avoid: lifting the hips, jerking, fast drop.

Leg extension machine (quads)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Pad on the front of the lower shins, knees aligned with the pivot. Straighten the legs, squeeze the quads, lower slowly. Cue: pause at the top, no hard knee lock. Avoid: swinging, fast drop, too heavy on the knees.

Dumbbell Romanian deadlift (RDL)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Hinge at the hips with a flat back (left). The moment the back rounds (right) — stop and reset.

Feet hip-width, knees slightly bent (and they stay bent). Lower the dumbbells down the front of the legs to a strong hamstring stretch (~mid-shin), then drive the hips forward to stand. Cue: weights close to the legs, flat back throughout. Avoid: rounding the back (#1 injury risk), squatting instead of hinging, weights drifting away.

Hip thrust (barbell/machine)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
At the top, knees-to-shoulders form a straight line and the shins are vertical — squeeze the glutes, don't over-arch the back.

Upper back on a bench, padded bar across the hips, feet flat. Drive through the heels to full hip extension, lower slowly. Avoid: arching the back instead of squeezing glutes, pushing through the toes, half reps.

Calf raise (standing/seated)

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Balls of the feet on a platform, heels free. Rise up as high as possible, squeeze, lower the heels slowly below the platform for a full stretch. Avoid: tiny bouncy reps, no bottom stretch, rushing.

Core

Plank

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos
Forearms under the shoulders; hips level — no sag, no pike.

Avoid: sagging hips, raised butt, holding the breath, craning the neck up.

Side plank

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

On one forearm, body sideways in a straight line, feet stacked. Lift the hips to a straight line, hold, switch sides. Cue: shoulder over elbow, hips lifted. Avoid: hips dropping, rotating forward, elbow ahead of the shoulder.

Hanging or lying leg raises

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Hang from a bar, or lie flat with hands under the hips. Legs fairly straight, raise using the abs, lower slowly. Cue: control the lowering; keep the lower back pressed down (lying). Avoid: swinging, arching the lower back, using hip flexors instead of abs.

Cable / machine crunch

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Kneel facing a high cable with a rope behind the head (or use the ab-crunch machine). Crunch down by curling the spine — ribs toward pelvis — using the abs; return slowly. Avoid: hinging at the hips only, pulling with the arms, going too fast.

Russian twists

▶ Watch tutorials🖼 See photos

Sit, knees bent, heels down or lifted, torso leaned back ~45°, light weight at the chest. Rotate to tap toward the floor on each side. Cue: rotation from the torso/obliques, chest up. Avoid: moving only the arms, hard rounding of the back, going too fast.

When to ask for help. Unsure a machine fits your body? Ask gym staff to check your positioning once — a 30-second adjustment saves weeks of strain. On the most form-sensitive lifts (Romanian deadlift, hip thrust, lunges), film a set from the side once to confirm your back stays flat and knees track properly.
